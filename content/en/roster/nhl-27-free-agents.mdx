<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Contract Negotiation Guide">

Securing top-tier talent requires more than just throwing money at players. You must manage their interest levels, evaluate competing offers from rival GMs, and structure deals that protect your future salary cap.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Assess Roster Needs">
    Review your team's positional depth, salary cap space, and upcoming rookie extensions before the free agency window opens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Filter the Market">
    Sort the free agents list by OVR, age, and position. Add your top targets to your watchlist to monitor competing offers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Analyze Player Interest">
    Check the player's interest meter. Players with high interest in your franchise are more likely to accept team-friendly contracts.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Structure the Offer">
    Adjust the contract length and salary. Offer front-loaded or back-loaded structures depending on your future cap projections.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Submit and Monitor">
    Submit the offer and advance the calendar day by day. Be ready to increase your offer if a rival team submits a higher bid.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Negotiation Hack">
If a player has multiple offers, you can often win the sweepstakes without increasing the annual salary by adding a No-Movement Clause (NMC) or adding an extra year to the contract duration.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Salary Cap and Roster Management Strategies">

Managing the salary cap is the most challenging aspect of Franchise Mode. Signing marquee players can instantly turn your team into a contender, but overspending will force you to trade away valuable young prospects.

| Strategy Type | Best For | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Win Now** | Contenders | Maximizes current roster strength | Creates severe cap issues in future years |
| **Bridge Deals** | Developing Teams | Keeps young stars under control | Players demand massive raises later |
| **Veteran Minimums** | Cap-Strapped Teams | Fills depth roles for under $1M | Low on-ice impact, no trade value |

<Callout type="info" title="Cap Compliance Rule">
Always leave at least $2.0M to $3.0M in cap space entering the regular season. This flexibility allows you to absorb contracts at the trade deadline or call up injured players from your AHL affiliate.
</Callout>
