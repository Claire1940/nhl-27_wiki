<Section icon={Map} title="How to Set Up Your Franchise Mode with Custom Teams">

Customizing your league provides a fresh experience away from standard NHL structures. The custom league feature allows you to adjust the number of divisions, modify schedule lengths, and introduce custom expansion franchises. This setup is ideal for players who want to create historical simulations, European-style relegation leagues, or massive 48-team mega-leagues.

Creating a balanced custom league requires careful attention to detail during the initial creation process. Setting incorrect parameters can lead to severe schedule congestion, unrealistic player generation, or broken salary cap progression in later seasons.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select League Structure">
    Choose between the standard 32-team format or expand the league up to 48 teams. Adjust division alignments to ensure balanced travel schedules and realistic divisional rivalries.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Expansion Settings">
    Determine if you want to run an expansion draft. If enabled, customize the protection lists to decide how many forwards, defensemen, and goaltenders existing teams can protect from selection.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Adjust Simulation Sliders">
    Modify trade frequency, injury occurrences, and prospect draft class quality. Setting draft class quality to medium ensures a steady flow of talent without oversaturating the league with elite prospects.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize Roster Assignments">
    Assign custom rosters or run a fantasy draft to completely randomize player distribution across all active franchises before starting the regular season simulation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Configuration Warning">
Expanding the league to 48 teams significantly dilutes the free-agent pool. Ensure you increase the prospect draft class quality slightly to compensate for the higher demand for roster-caliber players.
</Callout>

When configuring your expansion draft, the rules you select will dictate how quickly your new franchise can become competitive. Strict protection rules force you to build through draft picks, while lenient rules allow you to select high-quality depth players immediately.

| Setting | Recommended Option | Gameplay Impact |
| **Salary Cap** | On | Forces realistic roster construction and budgeting |
| **Draft Class Quality** | Medium | Prevents rapid generation of overpowered rookies |
| **Player Morale** | Off | Reduces artificial locker room chemistry drops |
| **CPU Trade Difficulty** | Hard | Prevents exploiting AI teams for easy draft picks |
