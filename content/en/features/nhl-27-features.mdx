<Section icon={Trophy} title="Connected Franchise: The Core of NHL 27 Features">

The suite of **nhl 27 features** marks a massive turning point for the franchise, headlined by the long-awaited return of Connected Franchise. This mode has sat at the absolute top of community wishlists for years, and its first iteration establishes a robust, modern foundation designed for social play, intense competition, and living league dynamics. Built with extensive community playtesting and user experience research, the mode aims to eliminate the navigation friction that plagued previous online franchise attempts.

**Video Highlights:**
- **GM Connected Returns**: Play in customizable leagues with up to 32 human-controlled teams.
- **Commission Tools**: Full suite for managing rosters, resolving disputes, and advancing phases.
- **Crossplay Support**: Roster sharing and league play are fully compatible across consoles.
- **Flexible Schedules**: Run leagues at your own pace with adjustable season lengths.

Connected Franchise allows players to build custom leagues with 3 to 32 human-controlled teams. To preserve competitive balance, the mode focuses heavily on human-versus-human interaction. While custom leagues can be populated with fewer than 32 players, any remaining spots are filled by passive teams that operate on the side to maintain realistic standings without disrupting the human economy. Roster sharing, custom draft classes, and offline creation tools are fully integrated with crossplay, ensuring that players on different platforms can participate in the same customized hockey universe.

| Feature Option | Settings Range | Functional Benefit |
| League Size | 3 to 32 players | Accommodates both small friend groups and massive online communities |
| Roster Customization | Default or offline custom | Supports custom-created rosters and shared community files |
| Schedule Flexibility | 15 to 82 games | Allows commissioners to shorten seasons to speed up progression |
| Commissioner Tools | Full override suite | Enables trade vetoes, roster overrides, and manual game resolutions |

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Exploit Design">
To prevent players from taking advantage of AI logic, leagues with fewer than 32 human players restrict direct trading and gameplay matchups against non-human teams. This ensures league integrity and prevents artificial stat-padding.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Authentic Atmospheres and Broadcast Upgrades">

The visual and auditory presentation has been completely rebuilt to deliver what developers call "hockey tourism." Every arena now features highly specific, authentic details that reflect the home team's identity. From the dynamic castle and dragon animations in Las Vegas to authentic goal songs, crowd chants, and custom arena lighting, playing on the road feels like a genuine challenge.

At the center of this presentation overhaul is the Orchestrator system. This engine monitors the flow of the game and dynamically coordinates the arena's response. Instead of relying on repetitive, pre-rendered sequences, the Orchestrator instantly adjusts crowd volume, arena lighting, jumbotron graphics, and power ring displays to match the emotional high points of the match, such as a game-saving breakaway stop or a late-period goal.

| Presentation Element | Old System | NHL 27 Orchestrator |
| Crowd Reactions | Static cheering loops | Dynamic scaling based on game context and momentum |
| Arena Lighting | Uniform brightness | Dynamic, team-specific light shows during intros and goals |
| Jumbotrons | Generic stat displays | Modern, broadcast-style graphics and real-time replay packages |
| Broadcast Scorebugs | Outdated designs | Clean, minimalist overlays inspired by modern sports networks |

A brand-new commentary team also enters the booth, featuring John Buccigross and Darren Pang. Buccigross brings signature play-by-play energy, while Darren Pang provides authentic analytical insights from his perspective as a former NHL goaltender. This combination replaces the long-running commentary style with a modern, analytical broadcast feel.

<Callout type="tip" title="Home-Ice Advantage">
Crowds are now highly reactive to momentum. If you are the away team, scoring an early goal will visibly quiet the crowd, while letting the home team build momentum will cause the crowd to roar, actively putting pressure on your players.
</Callout>

</Section>

Hockey Ultimate Team (HUT) players receive a major boost in strategic depth with the introduction of authentic NHL coaches and playbooks. Rather than relying on generic, fictional coaches, players can now recruit real-world bench bosses to lead their custom rosters. These coaches bring their actual historical strategies and playbooks, which directly impact how your team positions itself on the ice.

This coaching integration adds a brand-new layer to team building. Line chemistry is no longer just about matching player types; it now requires aligning your roster with your coach's historical preferences. For example, deploying players who have thrived under a specific coach's system in real life will yield significant chemistry bonuses, boosting attributes like passing, defensive awareness, and shooting accuracy.

<Callout type="warning" title="Playbook Consistency">
