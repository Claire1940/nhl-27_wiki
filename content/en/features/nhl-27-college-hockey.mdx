While Hockey Ultimate Team features direct integration of licensed NCAA cards, offline modes like Franchise Mode and Be a Pro utilize a mix of licensed prospects and generic stand-ins depending on the player's draft status and individual licensing agreements.

| Mode | Licensing Status | Custom Roster Support | Headshot Availability |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| Franchise Mode | Partial coverage via draft pools | Yes, fully customizable | No, generic player avatars |
| Be a Pro | Limited to drafted prospects | Yes, via roster sharing | No, generic player avatars |

The variation in licensing means that while you will see top-tier prospects like Gavin McKenna wearing Penn State gear in HUT, their representation in offline franchise modes may require manual adjustments or community-created roster files to achieve full realism.

<Section icon={Shield} title="CHL to NCAA Transition Rules">

A major factor driving the inclusion of collegiate players is the real-world shift in development pathways. Historically, players in the Canadian Hockey League (CHL) could not transition to NCAA play without losing their amateur eligibility. Recent rule changes have altered this dynamic, allowing players to move between major junior leagues and the collegiate system.

| League Source | Projected Draft Share | Development Path | Game Representation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| NCAA College | High (approx 50% of round 1) | Collegiate development | Mixed (HUT and custom rosters) |
| CHL (OHL/WHL/QMJHL) | Moderate (decreased share) | Major junior development | Fully licensed default rosters |
| European Leagues | Stable | Professional development | Fully licensed default rosters |

This change has a direct impact on how draft classes are generated in Franchise Mode. With more top-tier talent opting for the NCAA route, the draft pool in the game must reflect this shift to maintain realism. Players who previously would have been locked to CHL rosters are now appearing as collegiate commits, changing how scout scouting budgets should be allocated.
