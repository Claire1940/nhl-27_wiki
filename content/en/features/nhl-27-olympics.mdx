<Section icon={Swords} title="Step-by-Step International Tournament Guide">

Winning the gold medal requires a methodical approach to tournament play. Follow this step-by-step progression route to set up your team, master the wider ice surface, and defeat elite international opponents.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select and Customize Your Team">
    Choose your national team and access the roster management screen. Ensure your lines are set up to maximize chemistry, pairing elite playmakers with high-end snipers who can finish cross-crease opportunities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Your Tactical Strategies">
    Open your team strategies menu. Set your forecheck to a conservative 1-2-2 Passive to avoid getting caught deep in the offensive zone. Adjust your defensive system to Collapsing to protect the high-danger slot area.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Master the Wide-Ice Breakout">
    During breakouts, utilize the extra space behind your net. Use quiet zones along the boards to draw the opponent's forecheckers in before executing a crisp breakout pass to your transitioning center.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute Controlled Zone Entries">
    Avoid dumping the puck in. Instead, use diagonal passes to trailing wingers or utilize lateral dekes to carry the puck across the blue line. Keep possession until a clean passing lane opens up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
Following this conservative, possession-first structure will drastically reduce the number of odd-man rushes you concede, keeping your goaltender protected even against high-OVR offenses.
</Callout>
