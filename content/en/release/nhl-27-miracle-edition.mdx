<Section icon={Swords} title="ICE-Q Gameplay Innovations & Beginner Controls">

On-ice realism reaches a new peak with the refinement of the ICE-Q 2.0 engine. Powered by real-world NHL EDGE tracking data, this system dynamically adjusts player skating acceleration, top speed, and turning radiuses based on individual player attributes. Skaters move with authentic momentum, making positioning and tactical awareness far more important than simple speed-burst spamming. 

Furthermore, defensive players will notice smarter spacing and physical play, while the Goalie Crease Control system introduces over 80 new save animations. Goaltenders react dynamically to deflections, screen situations, and cross-crease passes, making scoring a rewarding tactical challenge. The On-Ice Trainer dynamically shows passing lanes and shooting targets, helping beginners recognize when a goalie is out of position.

To master these new systems, beginners should familiarize themselves with the core control schemes. We recommend starting with Hybrid controls to master the basics, then transitioning to the Skill Stick for maximum creative control over your puck handling.

To build muscle memory before jumping into competitive online matches, follow our structured practice routine.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Optimize Settings and Camera">
    Set your difficulty to Rookie, enable the On-Ice Trainer to Light or Full, and select the Overhead or Zone camera angle. This setup provides the best possible view of passing lanes and defensive positioning while giving you extra reaction time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Skating and Puck Protection">
    Enter Free Skate mode. Practice skating complete laps without holding down the hustle modifier. Use the left stick for tight turns and hold L2/LT to activate Vision Control, keeping your hips turned toward the net.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Drill Passing and Shooting Lanes">
    In Free Skate, complete ten consecutive standard passes, followed by five saucer passes. Transition into shooting drills: practice moving the goaltender laterally using minor right-stick movements before releasing a quick wrist shot into the open corner.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defend Without Taking Penalties">
    Play a single offline Play Now match on Rookie difficulty. Focus entirely on staying positioned between the puck carrier and your net. Use poke checks and stick lifts only when the opponent's stick is within immediate reach to avoid tripping or slashing penalties.
  </Step>
</Steps>
